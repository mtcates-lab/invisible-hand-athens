Nodes

A node is any organization, collective, mutual aid group, faith community, or resource provider doing consistent community work in Athens, GA.

No 501c3 required. Nodes range from registered nonprofits to informal groups with no legal status. What matters is that they show up consistently and are accountable in some form to the community they serve.

The size of each node on the map reflects how many connections it has — larger nodes are more connected to the rest of the network.

Edges

An edge is a documented relationship between two organizations. Edges are what make this a network rather than just a list — they show how the community ecosystem actually functions.

Edge types: Referral (one org sends people to another), Partnership (active collaboration), Resource flow (material resources move between orgs), Shared population (both serve the same people), Overlap (similar work in same area), Donates to (a business or provider donates food or resources).

Missing edges are just as important as existing ones — they reveal connections that should exist but don't yet.

Gaps 5 identified

A gap is a known hole in the Athens community network — a service that doesn't exist, a geographic area with no coverage, an org doing important work that nobody knows about, or a connection between orgs that should exist but doesn't.

Gaps are not failures. They're information. Mapping them is the first step toward filling them — either by finding existing orgs that address the gap, facilitating new connections, or surfacing the need to the community.
